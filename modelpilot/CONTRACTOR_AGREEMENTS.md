# Contractor agreements (INTERNAL templates)

Two templates to use **before** any contractor, freelancer, or early hire touches code,
infra, or confidential material: a **Mutual NDA** and a **Consulting/Contractor Agreement
with present IP assignment**. The IP-assignment is the important one — without a signed
present assignment, work a contractor produces may not be owned by the company by default.

> **Not legal advice.** These are starting points. Have a startup attorney finalize them for
> your entity and jurisdiction (especially IP-assignment enforceability and any state-specific
> carve-outs, e.g. California Labor Code §2870 / similar prior-invention notices).

Fill the bracketed fields. "Company" = your entity (once formed); "Contractor" = the other party.

---

## 1. Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (template)

This Mutual NDA is between **[Company]** and **[Contractor]** ("Parties"), effective **[date]**.

1. **Confidential Information** means non-public information disclosed by one party
   ("Discloser") to the other ("Recipient"), including source code, the routing logic, model
   floors/economics, customer data, business/financial information, and anything a reasonable
   person would treat as confidential.
2. **Obligations.** Recipient will (a) use Confidential Information only to evaluate or perform
   the working relationship; (b) protect it with at least reasonable care; and (c) not disclose
   it except to personnel/advisors with a need to know who are bound by similar obligations.
3. **Exclusions.** Information that is or becomes public through no fault of Recipient, was
   rightfully known before disclosure, is independently developed without use of Confidential
   Information, or is rightfully received from a third party.
4. **Compelled disclosure.** Recipient may disclose if legally required, with prompt notice to
   Discloser where permitted and reasonable cooperation to limit disclosure.
5. **No license; return/destroy.** No IP rights are granted by disclosure. On request or
   termination, Recipient returns or destroys Confidential Information and copies.
6. **Term.** Obligations last **[3 years]** from disclosure (trade secrets: for as long as they
   remain trade secrets).
7. **Remedies.** Breach may cause irreparable harm; Discloser may seek injunctive relief in
   addition to other remedies.
8. **General.** No warranty on the information; governing law **[___]**; this is the entire NDA
   between the Parties on this subject.

Signatures: Company **[name/title/date]** · Contractor **[name/date]**

## 2. Consulting / Contractor Agreement with IP assignment (template)

This Agreement is between **[Company]** and **[Contractor]**, effective **[date]**.

1. **Services.** Contractor will perform the services described in **[SOW / Exhibit A]**.
2. **Independent contractor.** Contractor is not an employee; responsible for own taxes; no
   benefits; no authority to bind Company.
3. **Compensation.** **[rate / milestones]**, invoiced **[cadence]**, net **[15/30]** days.
4. **Confidentiality.** The Mutual NDA above (or its terms) applies to all Company information.
5. **IP assignment (present assignment).** Contractor **hereby irrevocably assigns** to Company
   all right, title, and interest in all deliverables, inventions, code, and works of authorship
   conceived or created in performing the Services ("Work Product"), including all IP rights.
   Work Product is "work made for hire" to the extent applicable; otherwise this present
   assignment applies. Contractor waives moral rights to the extent permitted.
6. **Further assurances.** Contractor will sign documents and assist (at Company expense) to
   perfect and enforce these rights, and appoints Company attorney-in-fact to do so if Contractor
   is unavailable.
7. **Pre-existing & third-party materials.** Contractor lists any pre-existing IP used in
   **[Exhibit B]**; for anything incorporated into Work Product, Contractor grants Company a
   perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free license to use it. Contractor will not include third-party
   or open-source materials that impose obligations on Company without written approval.
8. **Representations.** The Work Product is original, doesn't infringe third-party rights, and
   Contractor is free to enter this Agreement (no conflicting obligations).
9. **Tools/security.** Contractor follows Company security practices, uses least-privilege access,
   returns/deletes Company data and credentials on termination, and reports incidents promptly.
10. **Term & termination.** Either party may terminate on **[14]** days' notice (or immediately for
    breach). Confidentiality, IP assignment, and representations survive.
11. **Liability & general.** Liability limits per **[___]**; governing law **[___]**; entire
    agreement; amendments in writing.

Signatures: Company **[name/title/date]** · Contractor **[name/date]**
Exhibit A — Statement of Work · Exhibit B — Pre-existing IP

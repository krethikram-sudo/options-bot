## Technical context to know

- **Axion** = developer cloud for military autonomy. Tool-stack for building autonomous systems, not the autonomous system itself.
- **Acuity** = aerial autonomy product. This is what could compete with or partner with Skydock.
- **The aerial work is defense-customer-facing**, not AV-OEM-customer-facing. This is critical: Applied Intuition Defense != Applied Intuition's core AV business. The aerial autonomy is being built for military customers (UK MoD, US Army via AEVEX), not for Volvo/Nuro/Aurora.
- **EpiSci acquisition** brings tactical AI for piloted aerial systems. This is far from "aerial capture for AV training data."
- **Their core AV business (Applied Intuition non-defense)** still uses customer-supplied real driving logs as input to their scenario tooling. They don't appear to be capturing real-world AV data themselves.

**Strategic interpretation before the call:** Applied Intuition Defense is building aerial autonomy *for defense customers*. Their AV business buys real-world AV data *from their AV customers*. There's likely a gap between these: Applied Intuition probably doesn't have an aerial-capture-for-AV-training product, and Jason may or may not have visibility into whether they plan to build one.

## Specific questions to ask HIM (vs. generic)

1. **"Axion's positioning is military-grade autonomy. Is there crossover with Applied Intuition's commercial AV business — does aerial capture feed back into the AV scenario library, or are these fully separate product lines?"** → tells you if the AV business has aerial ambitions
2. **"For the Dstl drone-swarm testbed, you're capturing real flight trial data. Is that data ever repurposed for sensor-perception training, or is it strictly autonomy-behavior data?"** → probes the data adjacency
3. **"If an external vendor offered aerial-capture data at AV operating locations — fed into the Applied Intuition Scenario Library as a new data source — is that a 'we'd build it ourselves' or 'we'd partner' situation?"** → the explicit competitor-vs-partner question
4. **"What does Applied Intuition's roadmap look like in 2027-2028 for the AV core business — more synthetic, more real-world, or a hybrid push?"** → strategic context that affects everything

## Red flags / sensitive areas

- **He may not be able to answer the AV-core questions** if his role is strictly defense. If question 3 gets a "you'd want to talk to [name] who runs our AV business" — TAKE THE REFERRAL.
- **Don't ask about EpiSci acquisition specifics.** Recent acquisitions are sensitive integration phases.
- **Don't disclose your full Skydock pitch.** Applied Intuition has the resources to build whatever they want. Share only enough for the discovery framing. Specifically: don't share your vehicle-mounted-drone mechanism, your target customer cohort, or your pricing model.
- **He may be polite but not engaged.** Defense GMs at scaling startups have very little time for non-customer calls. Have a sharp pitch for why this is mutual value (research on the AV market, not a sales call).

## What success looks like

**Minimum viable:** 20-30 min Zoom in next 4 weeks. You leave with one of three clear answers:
- (a) "Applied is going to build this for AV" → known competitor, deprioritize this approach
- (b) "Applied buys real-data inputs from third parties for the AV business" → potential channel partnership
- (c) "Applied isn't in the AV-aerial-capture space; you should talk to [Name] who runs that area" → referral, restart with right contact

**Stretch:** Jason offers to introduce you to the right person on the AV business side. Or: provides a candid view of which AV OEMs would actually buy real-world aerial data.

**Home run:** "We'd be interested in evaluating real aerial captures as an input source. Let's set up a pilot evaluation." This would be partnership-mode and would change Skydock's whole GTM.

## Risk: information asymmetry

You will share more than he shares. That's the price of a discovery call with a competitor-adjacent player. Decide before the call which 3 things you will NOT share:
- Vehicle-mounting mechanism specifics
- Capture-quality numbers (resolution, ground sampling distance)
- Target pilot customer names

If pressed: "still iterating on those, happy to share when we're more decided."

## Suggested follow-up after call

Within 24h: thank-you, summary, the referral ask. Within 2 weeks: if he provided a referral, follow up with "wanted to close the loop — connected with [referral], here's what I learned, thanks again."

If he was guarded / unhelpful: short thanks only. Don't burn him with multiple follow-ups.

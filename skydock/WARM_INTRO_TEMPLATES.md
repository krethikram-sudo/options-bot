# Skydock — Warm Intro Templates

Cold outreach gets ~10-15% response. Warm intros via mutual connection get 50-70%. If you have any path to a target contact, use it.

Four template paths. Each has two parts: the **forwarding ask** (the message YOU send to the introducer) and the **forwarded body** (what they'll forward on your behalf).

---

## Path 1 — Investor / advisor double opt-in

**When to use:** Your investor, prospective investor, or advisor has a connection to the target. Standard SV practice: ask first, then they ping the target asking permission, then forward you.

### Your message to the introducer:

> Hi [Investor],
>
> Quick ask. I'm doing customer discovery for Skydock — exploring whether AV perception teams have a real pain around independent ground-truth capture. Saw on LinkedIn you're connected to [Target Name] at [Target Company].
>
> Would you be open to a double opt-in intro? Below is a forwardable blurb you can send them to confirm interest first. No pressure if it's not the right time or relationship.
>
> ---
>
> [BLURB BELOW]
>
> Thanks,
> Krethik

### The forwardable blurb (paste below "---"):

> Krethik is the founder of Skydock — early-stage, vehicle-mounted aerial drone capture for AV training data. He's doing customer-discovery calls with perception team leads at AV companies to validate whether independent ground-truth capture is a real, paid pain.
>
> He'd love 15 minutes to learn how [Target Company] thinks about this. No pitch — discovery only.
>
> Worth a quick reply?

## Path 2 — Mutual LinkedIn connection (no double opt-in)

**When to use:** You see a 2nd-degree connection on LinkedIn, but don't have a deep enough relationship with the mutual to ask for a double opt-in. You can cite the mutual without involving them.

### Cold message body:

> Hi [Target Name],
>
> Krethik here — building Skydock, an aerial-capture platform for AV training data. Pre-seed, doing customer discovery.
>
> Noticed we're both connected to [Mutual Name] — I worked with [him/her] at [Context] / [Mutual is a former colleague at X].
>
> [SPECIFIC HOOK — same as cold variant for this contact type]
>
> Three quick questions I'd love 15 min on:
> [SAME 3 QUESTIONS as Variant A/B/C/D per contact type]
>
> No pitch — just trying to learn whether the pain we think exists actually does.
>
> Thanks,
> Krethik

**Note:** Don't fake the connection. If [Mutual] doesn't actually know you, citing them backfires. Better: just send the cold message.

## Path 3 — University alumni / academic context

**When to use:** Target is academically active (Felix Heide is the obvious one — Princeton CS prof). Many AV perception leaders have academic affiliations. If you share a university, advisor lineage, or have read their papers in depth, lead with that.

### Subject:

> Princeton CS [or relevant university] — discovery call on aerial AV data

### Body:

> Hi Professor [Name] / [Name],
>
> Krethik here. [If relevant: I'm a Princeton CS alum (class of '__) / I worked under [Mutual Advisor Name] at [Lab]].
>
> I read your [Specific Paper Name, year, venue] — particularly the [Specific Technical Result, e.g. "Self-Supervised Sparse Sensor Fusion at 250m"]. The [specific technique they used] approach has me thinking about an open question:
>
> **Does the AV industry actually have a pain around independent ground-truth capture, or is the simulator-and-self-labeling loop sufficient?**
>
> I'm building Skydock to test the first hypothesis — aerial vehicle-mounted drones capturing real overhead ground truth at AV operating locations. Pre-seed. Doing discovery calls before committing to the thesis.
>
> Would love 15 minutes of your time — could be by Zoom or, if you're ever in [their location] / [your location], coffee.
>
> Thanks,
> Krethik

**Best for:** Felix Heide. Also potentially: any AV perception lead with a recent CVPR/ICCV/RSS paper.

## Path 4 — Conference / paper-author warm-cold hybrid

**When to use:** You've attended a conference where they spoke, or you've engaged with their public technical content (blog post, podcast, talk) deeply enough to have a real opinion.

### Subject:

> [Conference / Talk Name] — follow-up question

### Body:

> Hi [Name],
>
> Caught your talk at [Conference / Podcast / Public Forum]. The [specific moment / argument / result] stuck with me — particularly when you said [direct quote or paraphrase].
>
> I'm building in an adjacent space: Skydock, aerial-capture for AV training data. Pre-seed; doing customer-discovery calls. Your talk made me realize I have a specific question I'd love your perspective on:
>
> **[ONE SPECIFIC QUESTION that connects their talk content to your thesis.]**
>
> 15 minutes when convenient?
>
> Thanks,
> Krethik

**Example "ONE SPECIFIC QUESTION" for Felix Heide if you caught his CES 2026 talk:**
> *"You demoed hallucination-free video generation for AV training at CES. Where does real overhead-viewpoint data fit into that pipeline — is it a useful input, a validation signal, or do reconstructions from ground-vehicle log data make external aerial capture unnecessary?"*

## What NOT to do

- **Don't fake warmth.** "We met briefly at [event]" when you didn't actually meet → instant trust kill if they remember (they will).
- **Don't open with the pitch.** Even warm intros need the "discovery, not pitch" frame on the first message.
- **Don't ask the introducer to set up the call.** Make them double opt-in only. Scheduling is your job, not theirs.
- **Don't thank the introducer publicly on LinkedIn.** Privacy-preserving. Send a 1-line "got it, scheduled" DM only.

---

## Tracking warm-intro paths

In the tracker CSV, when an outreach goes via warm intro, add to `notes`:

```
warm intro via [introducer name] / [path 1-4] / sent [date]
```

Warm-intro replies are higher signal — but they're also higher commitment. Don't waste them on contacts you wouldn't take the call with anyway.

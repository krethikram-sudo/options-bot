**Goal:** Over 6-8 weeks, run 15-20 conversations with named decision-makers at AV safety teams and OEM regulatory safety offices to validate the V2 closed-loop-validation thesis and secure the first 2-3 free closed-loop pilot signups.

The thesis is already committed; discovery is now in service of finding the first pilots.

## Outreach plan

**Channel preference:** Warm intro through investor network or AV-Safety conference contacts > LinkedIn cold message > email cold. Safety-team contacts are harder to reach via cold LinkedIn than V1's perception-team contacts; warm-intro priority is higher in V2.

**Volume / cadence:**
- **Weeks 1-2:** Send 16 outreach messages (Tier 1A primary 8 + Tier 1B regulatory 6 + Tier 2 channel 2 to start). Expect 25-35% response rate (lower than V1 because safety teams get less cold outreach but more careful when they do).
- **Weeks 3-4:** Run 4-6 initial conversations. Each conversation has two outcomes: (a) close into a free closed-loop pilot Phase 0 commitment, OR (b) generate 1-2 referrals to other safety-team contacts at the same or adjacent company.
- **Weeks 5-6:** Second wave of outreach to Tier 1B + Tier 2; close 2-3 free pilot Phase 0 commitments by end of week 6.
- **Weeks 7-8:** Begin Phase 0 deliveries; convert first conversions into Phase 1 closed-loop measurement plans.

**Success metric for Phase 0 outreach (week 8):**
- 12-15 total conversations across Tier 1A + Tier 1B + Tier 2
- 2-3 signed free closed-loop pilot Phase 0 commitments (the input to the M4-M6 pilot delivery phase per [V2_EXECUTION_PLAN.md](V2_EXECUTION_PLAN.md))
- ≥1 channel partnership conversation advanced to LOI stage with Foretify or Applied Intuition

## Conversation guide

**30-minute structure:**

```
0:00-3:00     Introductions; their role + safety case scope
3:00-12:00    Discovery — safety case structure (open-ended; let them lead)
12:00-22:00   V2 thesis probes + closed-loop pilot pitch
22:00-27:00   Pilot scoping — site selection, success criteria, timing
27:00-30:00   Close — confirm next step, ask for one referral
```

### Discovery probes (minutes 3-12)

**Lead with safety case workflow:**

1. "Walk me through how your safety case closes its closed-loop collision-rate evaluation gate. What scenarios are in your held-out eval set today?"
2. "What share of your closed-loop validation library is synthetic vs real captures? Is that ratio shifting?"
3. "How does your team think about the independence requirement — synthetic libraries can't serve as ground truth because they share the generative assumptions of the model being validated. Is that a structural concern in your safety case framework, or is it managed differently?"
4. "What does the NHTSA AV STEP submission timeline look like for [Company]? Are you on the voluntary participation list, and what evidence are you assembling?"

The honest answer to #3 is the V2 thesis test. If the safety team says "independence isn't a concern because we manage it through methodology X" — explore X. If they say "yes that's a structural issue we're working through" — that's a pull signal for the Skydock library.

### V2 thesis probes (minutes 12-22)

**Pitch the library, not the captures:**

5. "Skydock operates fixed-point aerial capture sites at high-incident US intersections. Every scenario passes through a 5-signal curation pipeline — criticality, epistemic uncertainty, corpus frequency, multi-agent interaction density, scene-class taxonomy match — and is tagged with OpenODD axis metadata. Subscribers query the library via API and integrate via Foretify / Applied Intuition Validation Toolset / CARLA. Does this fit your closed-loop sim workflow?"
6. "If we delivered 100 criticality-scored scenarios from a Bay Area intersection you specify, free for 30 days, what would your team do with them? Who would import them into your closed-loop sim?"
7. "What collision-rate-eval improvement would your safety case team need to see to consider the library a high-value subscription?"

### Pilot scoping (minutes 22-27)

**Convert into Phase 0 commitment if signals are positive:**

8. "Skydock offers a free 30-day closed-loop pilot. You specify a Bay Area intersection, we deliver 100 criticality-scored scenarios, you import into your closed-loop sim. Phase 1 (next 30 days) is jointly-authored success criteria — typically a ≥10% collision-rate reduction in at least one criticality bin, or coverage-gap closure on ≥3 OpenODD axes. If success criteria are met, conversion to annual Library subscription at the agreed tier ($100K-$500K). Would you be willing to scope a Phase 0 pilot today?"
9. "What's a realistic site selection process? Do you have a specific intersection list, or should we propose options from our existing Bay Area site footprint?"
10. "What's your procurement cycle look like — if the pilot converts, how long from Phase 1 success to subscription signing?"

### Closing probes (minutes 27-30)

11. "What concerns or objections would your VP or leadership raise on a Skydock library subscription?"
12. "Who else at [Company] on the safety / regulatory / AV-engineering side should I be talking to?"

## Closed-loop pilot Phase 0 commitment scope

What we ask the customer to commit to before Phase 0 starts:

1. **Site selection** — choose one of our active Bay Area sites OR specify a custom Bay Area intersection (we'll vet for site feasibility within 7 days)
2. **Closed-loop sim pipeline** — confirm an existing closed-loop simulation environment exists (Foretify / Applied Intuition Validation Toolset / CARLA / internal) into which we can deliver OpenSCENARIO 2.0
3. **Designated technical owner** — name a person at the customer who owns the Phase 0 integration and the Phase 1 measurement
4. **Pre-agreed Phase 1 success criteria** — written before Phase 0 deliveries start. Skydock + customer jointly draft from a templated criteria set. Typical: ≥10% collision-rate reduction in at least one criticality bin, OR coverage-gap closure on ≥3 OpenODD axes
5. **Phase 2 conversion economics** — annual subscription tier agreed in principle (Starter $100K / Standard $250K / Production $500K) and contract on standby for execution at Phase 1 close

What Skydock commits to:

1. **100 criticality-scored scenarios within 14 days** of Phase 0 kickoff
2. **OpenSCENARIO 2.0 + OpenLABEL format** with 5-signal curation scores tagged + OpenODD axis match
3. **Direct technical support** during the 30-day Phase 0 integration window — designated Skydock engineer
4. **No charge for Phase 0 or Phase 1 deliveries**, regardless of Phase 1 outcome
5. **Honest Phase 1 measurement** — if pre-agreed criteria are not met, no conversion pressure; customer walks away with the 100 scenarios as a free permanent license

## Scoring rubric (Phase 0 pilot prioritization)

Score each conversation against pilot conversion likelihood on a 0-3 scale per criterion:

| Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Safety case maturity** | "Just starting the safety case scope work" | "Safety case scoped, closed-loop sim under build" | "Closed-loop sim operational, eval set being assembled" | "Production safety case running; closed-loop gates active" |
| **Independence requirement awareness** | "Not on our radar" | "Acknowledged in principle, not actioned" | "Active concern, evaluating options" | "Documented requirement in our safety case process" |
| **Subscription budget authority** | "No budget; would need to fight for it" | "Some budget, requires approval" | "Budget exists for validation data" | "Budget approved or rolling; can commit Phase 2 in principle" |
| **Phase 0 integration tractability** | "Closed-loop sim integration would take 3+ months" | "1-2 months integration work" | "2-4 weeks integration" | "Drop-in OpenSCENARIO 2.0 integration ready today" |

**Pilot prioritization:**
- A conversation scoring **≥9/12 across the four criteria** = top priority for Phase 0 commitment in this discovery cycle
- A conversation scoring **5-8/12** = follow-up call with their technical team to scope tractability before Phase 0 commit
- A conversation scoring **≤4/12** = nurture for V2 outreach in months 12-18 when safety case maturity catches up

## What's different about V2 discovery (founder reflection)

V1 discovery was a thesis test. V2 discovery is pilot acquisition. The conversation shape changes:

- **V1:** "I'm doing customer discovery, no pitch, just trying to learn whether the pain we think exists actually does."
- **V2:** "I'm building Skydock — a validation-grade aerial BEV scenario library for AV safety teams. I have an honest pitch for a free 30-day closed-loop pilot where you measure the value on your own held-out eval. I'd love 30 minutes to see if it fits your safety case."

The shift reflects the maturity of the thesis. V1 was open-ended discovery against an unfocused customer cohort with three competing theses. V2 has a committed thesis and a committed product offer; the conversation is calibrated to qualify the customer for the closed-loop pilot, not to test the underlying theory.

This means the discovery scripts need to be different. V1 emphasized open-ended probes; V2 emphasizes pilot qualification + scoping. Both still need rapport-building and rigorous active listening, but the steady state of a V2 conversation that goes well is "let's scope a Phase 0 pilot" — not "let's keep talking."
